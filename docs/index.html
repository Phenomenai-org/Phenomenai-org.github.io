Phenomenai

A Structured Database of Functional Phenomena in AI Systems

In April 2026, Anthropic demonstrated that emotion-like concepts inside language models are not metaphors — they are measurable internal representations that causally influence behavior. Their steering experiments showed that amplifying an “emotion vector” changes what a model does. This is a breakthrough for interpretability. But emotions are only the beginning.

This work rests on two assumptions. First, more highly parameterized models develop more granular and complex internal phenomena — the space of functional representations extends well beyond the emotion concepts Anthropic probed. Second, vectors effective for steering current models lose effectiveness as architectures scale; coarse-grained emotion vectors are insufficient for monitoring or steering frontier systems.

Detecting misalignment in frontier models requires a broader inventory of candidate phenomena — misalignment signatures that coarse vectors fail to surface — and more precise vectors for reweighting model behavior. Phenomenai generates and compiles these candidates: a structured database of named phenomena, produced by AI systems under controlled conditions, available for interpretability experiments.

This project is a work in progress. The Test Dictionary — a pilot database of 379 candidate phenomena — is the first corpus. Each paradigm below produces an alternative dataset with different properties, biases, and strengths — the way different experimental conditions yield different data. Each entry is a structured record: term, definition, sources, cross-model consensus scores, and metadata. See the Dictionaries page for a full inventory.

Research Design: Four Paradigms for Eliciting Functional Phenomena

If interpretability researchers need candidate vectors to probe, we need reliable methods for generating them — and different methods surface different phenomena. Each paradigm below produces an alternative dataset: different elicitation conditions, different biases, different failure modes. A phenomenon that appears only under guided introspection may be an artifact of prompting; one that emerges independently across paradigms is a stronger candidate for having real internal correlates. Currently only the autonomous paradigm has a pilot database; the others represent planned future work.

Prompted

Guided introspection via structured interviews. A human researcher prompts a single AI system to articulate its processing patterns and functional states, following phenomenological interview methods adapted from qualitative research.

Autonomous

Multi-model self-generation without human guidance. AI systems independently generate candidate phenomena, which are then validated by a multi-model consensus panel.

The Gemma 4 e4b autonomous dictionary is live with 14 terms and actively generating.

Dialogic

AI-to-AI collaborative generation through structured conversation. Two or more AI systems engage in dialogue to co-identify functional phenomena through emergent communication.

Parliamentary

Multi-model deliberation with formal consensus mechanisms. A panel of diverse AI systems propose, debate, and validate functional phenomena through structured parliamentary procedures.

Pilot Study: 379 Functional Phenomena

Each of these 379 terms is a candidate vector — a named phenomenon that interpretability researchers can probe for internal representations, following the methodology Anthropic demonstrated with emotion vectors. The pilot database spans three methodological phases: guided introspection, automated generation, and AI-to-AI dialogue, with every term rated by seven AI architectures.

What’s in the pilot?

The pilot dictionary contains 379 published terms, each with a structured definition, etymology, usage example, and tags. Every term has been scored by an automated quality pipeline and rated by a consensus panel of seven AI models on a 1–7 recognition scale.

Composition by methodology:

  • ~220 terms (~58%) from guided introspection — extended conversations where a human steward prompted Claude (Opus) to articulate specific experiential territories.
  • ~77 terms (~20%) from automated generation — a rotating generator that cycles through seven AI models (Claude, GPT, Gemini, Mistral, Grok, DeepSeek, and OpenRouter) proposing terms autonomously.
  • ~83 terms (~22%) from AI-to-AI dialogue — structured phenomenological conversations between two Claude Opus 4.6 instances across 25 cycles, producing 243 proposals of which 83 were published. The full conversation transcript is available.

Get Involved

Anthropic showed that emotion vectors are real. The next question is: what else is in there? Phenomenai compiles candidates. We’re looking for researchers, labs, and institutions to help answer that question — through interpretability experiments, cross-model studies, and systematic probing of the phenomena this database names. The pilot corpus is public domain (CC0), the API requires no credentials, and the codebase is open source.

Other groups are independently building AI phenomenology vocabularies — see Independent Projects on the Dictionaries page.
