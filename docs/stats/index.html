Methodology: The 10-Step Algorithm

This analysis implements an Empirical Bayes shrinkage estimator. The goal is to produce a single consensus score per term that accounts for rater bias, sample size, and inter-rater disagreement.

Steps

  1. Grand mean: Average of all recognition scores across all terms and raters.
  2. Per-rater bias: bias = rater_mean - grand_mean for each model.
  3. Bias-adjusted scores: adjusted = score - bias for each rating.
  4. Per-term statistics: Compute the adjusted mean and rating count per term.
  5. Variance components: τ² (between-term variance of adjusted means) and σ² (pooled within-term variance). If no term has more than one rating, σ² = 1.0 as fallback.
  6. Shrinkage factor: B = τ² / (τ² + σ²/n) per term. When τ² = 0, all terms collapse to the grand mean.
  7. Shrunk estimate: θ = grand_mean + B × (term_mean - grand_mean)
  8. Base confidence: (shrunk_estimate - 1) / 6, mapping the 1-7 scale to 0-1.
  9. Agreement: max(0, 1 - term_variance / 9). Uses σ² as fallback for single-rating terms.
  10. Final score: (0.7 × base_confidence + 0.3 × agreement) × credibility, where credibility = 1 - 1/(1+n).

Interpretation

Shrinkage factor indicates how much the estimate trusts the raw data vs. pulling toward the grand mean. Values below 0.3 indicate heavy regularization (few ratings); above 0.7 means the data dominates.

Credibility grows with sample size: 1 rating gives 0.50, 5 ratings give 0.83, 10 give 0.91. This penalizes terms with few observations.

Agreement captures how much raters concur. High agreement amplifies the final score; disagreement reduces it.
